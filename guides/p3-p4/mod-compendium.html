Building CVMs using the Mod Compendium

What's the Mod Compendium?

The Mod Compendium is a program created by TGE that makes it extremely simple to create, install and combine multiple mods for Persona games. Currently, it only supports Persona 3 & 4 (PS2) and Persona 5 (PS3).

Download the Mod Compendium here.
In the case of Persona 3 & 4, the Mod Compendium generates CVM files and an ELF using your game's ISO. At the moment, you still need to use the premium version of UltraISO to save your new ISO.

Setting up the Mod Compendium
Once you've downloaded the program, unzip it using 7zip and extract the contents to a folder somewhere on your computer. Download new mods from the mods section of the site (make sure you're picking the Mod Compendium .zip format!)
Unzip your mods and put them in any subfolder of Mods in your Mod Compendium folder. You can organize them by the name of the game like shown, if you'd like. There should already be some sample mods in the folder for reference.

Building your modded CVMs

Start the program by running ModCompendium.exe (or click Refresh if you already have before adding your mods). They should all show up in the list when you choose Persona3 or Persona4 from the game dropdown menu in the top left corner.
Click on the Settings button and choose where you want your new CVM and ELF files to be saved. Also be sure to choose an unmodified ISO of your game, which the new CVMs and ELF will be based on.
Files in each mod will be merged together in the order of the list. In case two mods modify the same file, and you would prefer one over the other, you can click on a mod in the list to highlight it. Then, use the up/down buttons on the righthand side of the window to change its priority.
Finally, check the boxes on the left of each mod in the list to enable them and click Build! If all went well, your new files should be generated.
Open your game's original ISO in UltraISO and drag the newly generated files onto the window. Choose to replace the originals and Save As a new ISO. Note: if you don't have the premium version of UltraISO it won't let you save. UltraISO is the only program we know of at the moment that can create a bootable PS2 ISO.

Creating Your Own Mods
Click the New button to create a new mod entry in the list. Fill out the name, description, author and version (the rest is optional) and click OK. A new folder will be generated in the Mods folder of your Mod Compendium directory.
Add your modified files to a subfolder of Data in the newly generated folder named after your Mod.
Important: The contents of this directory must retain the same structure as the game's original CVMs. If you're unsure what folders your mods go in, check our Wiki or check the game's contents by extracting the game's original CPK files. For example, if you're making a new 01.ADX file, make sure it's in the Data/BGM folder of your mod like in the example.

Distributing Your Mods

While you could just share your resulting CVMs or make an xdelta patch, the point of the Mod Compendium is to avoid anyone having to unpack and create CPKs themselves in order to install and play mods. That said, you could simply zip up your mod's folder (the one containing the Data folder and mod.xml) and upload it to your choice of file host that way.

Other users can simply follow this guide to add your mod to their Mods folder.
